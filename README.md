<h1>
  <img src="https://github.com/user-attachments/assets/9b6ee75f-8585-4123-b4f8-f61312ec329b"
       width="54"
       style="vertical-align: middle; margin-right: 8px;">
  CodingCrops
</h1>

<p>
  <strong>CodingCrops</strong> is an accessibility-first farm simulator designed to introduce programming concepts through a game-based experience. Built with a strong focus on inclusive design, the project emphasizes keyboard navigation and screen reader support, making block-based coding more usable for learners with disabilities.
</p>

<p>
  🌱 Visit the app: <a href="https://codingcrops.com/">https://codingcrops.com/</a>
</p>

<p>
  CodingCrops transforms traditional block-based programming into an interactive farming environment where players use code-based logic to plant, grow, and harvest crops. By combining block coding with meaningful delivery of information via auditory feedback, the project helps learners build computational thinking skills while reducing barriers to participation.
</p>

<p>
  This project aims to:
</p>

<ul>
  <li>Lower barriers to entry for learners with disabilities</li>
  <li>Demonstrate best practices for accessible Blockly implementations</li>
  <li>Provide an engaging, game-based learning environment</li>
  <li>Support educators and organizations in inclusive STEM education</li>
</ul>

<p>
  CodingCrops is built on top of <a href="https://github.com/RaspberryPiFoundation/blockly">Blockly</a> (an open-source visual programming library) and it's <a href="https://github.com/RaspberryPiFoundation/blockly-keyboard-experimentation">accessibility plugin</a> (ongoing development).
</p>

<hr>

<h2>About Us</h2>
<p>
  CodingCrops is developed by <strong>Neil Squire Society’s <a href="https://www.makersmakingchange.com/">Makers Making Change (MMC)</a></strong>, a program dedicated to creating affordable, open-source assistive technologies. MMC works with volunteers, engineers, and community members to design solutions that improve independence and quality of life for people with disabilities.
</p>

<p>
  <strong><a href="https://www.neilsquire.ca/">Neil Squire Society</a></strong> is a Canadian non-profit organization that has been empowering people with disabilities through technology, education, and employment programs since 1984. Through Makers Making Change, the organization fosters innovation in accessibility by supporting projects like CodingCrops.
</p>

<hr>

<h2>Funding & Support</h2>
<p>
  We are a recipient of the <a href="https://developers.google.com/blockly/accessibility-fund-recipients">Blockly Accessibility Fund</a>, an initiative led by Google and are collaborating with the <strong>Raspberry Pi Foundation</strong> to improve the accessibility of Blockly. 
  Their support enables ongoing research, development, and testing to ensure inclusive design practices are integrated into modern coding environments.
</p>

<p>
  Our work includes improving screen reader compatibility, enhancing ARIA labeling, and addressing usability challenges for individuals with limited dexterity by providing feedback from the community.
</p>
